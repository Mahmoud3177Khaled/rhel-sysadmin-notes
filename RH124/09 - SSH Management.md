### SSH Management
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**SSH host keys** ---> SSH servers use public-key pairs to authenticate themselves to clients.

**ssh-keygen** --> Generate pub/private key pair saved in *.ssh/id_ed25519* and *.ssh/id_ed25519.pub*  
**ssh-keygen -l -f "pathToaPubKeyFile"** --> View the fingerprint of this pubkey for easy human comparison.  
**ssh-keygen -R serverb** --> remove the pub key i associate with what i think is serverb.  

**"/etc/ssh/ssh_host_*"** ---> Are my keys i send to people trying to ssh into me to prove to them i am who they think i am. They are automatically generated by sshd, if i wanna change them: **rm -f "/etc/ssh/ssh_host_*"**, then **systemctl restart sshd**  


#### How It goes when we SSH:

1. I wanna **ssh student@serverb** from servera
2. i got to the ip of who i think is serverb, and ask them to send thier pub key
3. I recieve their pub key, and see if i have verified before they really are serverb by 									looking in my **~/.ssh/known_hosts** then **/etc/ssh/ssh_known_hosts**:
		- I find their pubkey there --> great, they are who i thought were serverb!
		- I dont find that pubkey there --> Ok, they still might be who I think they are --> print 																							   their pub key to the user and he decides if thats the pub 																								   key of who i think is serverb or not... (yes, no, [fingerprint]):
																							- Yes --> From now on this is who i call serverb --> I save 																													  his pub key under ~/.ssh/known_hosts
																							- No --> Close connection, this is NOT serverb.

4. Great, I am now sure i am talking to serverb... now i need to prove to serverb i really am 				student...
5. I send my student pub key under **~/.ssh/id_ed25519.pub** to serverb for him to check me.
6. serverb checks if my pubkey exists under **~/.ssh/authorized_keys** on its end:
			- serverb finds it there --> Amazing, it is really student.
			- serverb doesnt find it there --> Ok, they still might be student but i didn't save his pub 																						key there... ---> Prompt him for his password:
																								- password correct --> Amazing! let him in!
																								- password incorrect --> Thats not student... close 																																									  connection.
7. Looking Great... you really are serverb and u are sure i really am student... now lets create a session key to encrypt our communication with, so none except us understand what we are saying!         Mission accomblished! Connection established!

8. hmm, but i really dont want to keep stating my password in step 6
	so from servera i run --> **ssh-copy-id student@serverb** to put myself my pub key from 																			 **.ssh/id_ed25519.pub** under **~/.ssh/authorized_keys**... so next 																		     time when he looks there he finds me and lets me in without a 																				     password :)

Also, **ssh-copy-id -i "PubKeyPath" serverb** ---> To specify which pubkey to copy to serverb.

Inside **~/.ssh/ssh_config** i can change the behavior of what i do if things go sideways... like:

**StrictHostKeyChecking** -->  What will i do if the pub key i recive from step 3 isn't the one i have saved?

*Options:*  
**yes** --> Refuses connection if key doesn't match. (Most secure, need manual host keys copying)  
**accept-new** --> Auto-accepts and stores **ONLY** new keys (else suspects MITM attack if keys mismatch, and closes connection).  
**ask** --> Prompts for confirmation on new keys. Refuses changed keys. (Default)  
**no** -->  Auto-accepts new keys **AND** changed keys.  (Least secure to MITM attacks).  

**ssh -o "setting"="option" >user>@>IP>** --> Temporarily change behavior one connection.  




**ssh-keyscan >IP> >> ~/.ssh/known_hosts** ---> I can also scan for a certain server, and save its pubkey in my known_hosts  
(risky, i dont check its really him...)  

**ssh-keyscan -t ed25519 serverb >> ~/.ssh/known_hosts** --> so only get the ed25519 key from serverb, and save it in known_hosts for future verification use.  

**ssh-keyscan 172.25.250.0/24 >> /etc/ssh/ssh_known_hosts** → Scan entire subnet for keys. and save them where users look if they didnt  a server using their known_hosts...  

⚠️ ssh-keyscan isfast but ONLY safe on trusted networks.   
On untrusted networks, verify fingerprints out-of-band (phone call, trusted person) before adding keys --- otherwise you're vulnerable to MITM during the scan itself.
